IG-01
"This is who you're asking…" team intro
Brands share employees' childhood photos with job titles — playful way to humanise teams and show the people behind the brand.
Simple format pairs adorable childhood photo with text overlay like "This is who you're asking to lead the Monday meeting" or "fix the website." Audiences love the juxtaposition of innocent kid photo with grown-up responsibility. Works because it softens corporate voice and drives comment engagement.
Doing it well

Service-based businesses

Travel & experience brands

LinkedIn

Carousel documents, short video, and comment-driven formats dominate as the algorithm rewards depth, dwell time, and authentic expertise.

LI-01
Carousel documents (PDF format)
Top engagement driver with 45.85% engagement rate — increases dwell time as users swipe through, making it algorithmic gold.
Carousels uploaded as multi-page PDFs force extended viewing time, which LinkedIn's algorithm interprets as strong engagement. Perfect for repurposing blog posts, frameworks, checklists, and case studies. Works because they combine visual storytelling with actionable takeaways that audiences save and share.
Doing it well
B2B SaaS & agencies
Creating step-by-step guides, data visualisations, and client results.
Thought leaders & consultants
Packaging frameworks and original research into swipeable formats.
HIGHEST ENGAGEMENT DWELL TIME REPURPOSABLE
LI-02
Native video under 90 seconds
Video views grew 36% YoY — short clips with strong hooks and captions deliver ~5.5% engagement, far outpacing text-only posts.
LinkedIn prioritises native video uploaded directly to platform over YouTube links. Videos under 90 seconds with captions, strong opening 3 seconds, and clear takeaways perform best. Algorithm rewards watch time and comment threads. Works because busy professionals consume video on mute during work hours.
Doing it well
Founders & executives
Sharing quick insights, lessons learned, and behind-the-scenes moments.
B2B marketers
Posting product demos, customer testimonials, and industry commentary.
VIDEO-FIRST 36% GROWTH MOBILE-NATIVE
LI-03
Comment-to-DM lead magnets
Posts offering guide/template in exchange for keyword comment drive engagement spikes — algorithm sees activity, poster builds qualified list.
Simple format: "Comment 'GUIDE' for my LinkedIn content template" triggers double benefit — boosts post into more feeds while building list of interested prospects. Comments increased 37% YoY and carry 8x more distribution weight than likes. Works because it's low-friction participation that signals intent.
Doing it well
Coaches & consultants
Offering frameworks, templates, and checklists to build pipeline.
SaaS & service providers
Using format to generate leads while expanding organic reach.

YouTube

Serialised formats, TV-quality long-form, and Shorts-to-long-form funnels define 2026 strategy as living room viewing grows.

YT-01
TV-style serialised content
Creators embrace episodic formats with recurring structures — viewers prefer channels with story arcs and predictable formats over one-offs.
YouTube's shift to TV screens (CTV) drives demand for binge-worthy series, multi-part explainers, and structured shows. Formats like "Coulda Been House" and "Speed Goes Pro" build recurring audiences. Works because serialisation creates anticipation and gives viewers reason to return between uploads.
Doing it well
IShowSpeed (Speed Goes Pro)
Turned sports chaos into structured episodic series with narrative arc.
Druski (Coulda Been House)
Big Brother-style format with creator chaos, limited episode runs for binge appeal.
EPISODIC TV-QUALITY BINGE-WORTHY
YT-02
Shorts as long-form discovery funnel
Shorts receive 200B daily views — best used as trailers for long-form content, not standalone strategy.
YouTube strengthening pathways between formats: Shorts dominate discovery and drive new audiences, but long-form builds loyalty and watch time. Smart creators use Shorts to tease, highlight moments, and direct to full videos. Works because it captures attention fast then converts to deeper engagement.
Doing it well
Educational & explainer channels
Using Shorts to preview concepts then driving to 10-20 min deep dives.
Entertainment creators
Posting reaction clips, best moments, and teasers to funnel to full episodes.
SHORTS STRATEGY DISCOVERY FUNNEL FORMAT
YT-03
Authentic, unfiltered content over polish
Over-produced videos now underperform — raw, unscripted, human content drives greater trust and engagement.
Shift toward lo-fi authenticity: Sam Sulek's unedited gym vlogs, Dry Creek Wrangler's uncut life lessons. Thumbnails getting simpler, edits less flashy, pacing more meandering. Algorithm rewards intimacy and viewer satisfaction over production budget. Works because audiences crave genuine connection over performance.
Doing it well
Sam Sulek
Unfiltered gym content with minimal editing, building massive loyal audience.
Dry Creek Wrangler School
Pivoted to uncut videos sharing life lessons, embracing raw storytelling.
