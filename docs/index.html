TikTok

03 TRENDS
TK — 01
Audio-led contrast humour
Heavy audio, trivial situation. The bigger the gap, the better it lands.
Two sounds running hard: Jocko Willink's "do you actually want to do this?" paired with minor inconveniences, and Charli XCX's "Boom Clap" for stories where something small got a surprisingly warm response. Quick to produce, no production value required.
Doing it well
Service businesses Client stories with a sharp punchline
Workplace creators "When a stakeholder asks for one small change"
Audio-driven Reactive ↑ High volume
TK — 02
Quiet flex + companion content
Authenticity is the brief. Overproduction is actively penalised right now.
Quiet flex: calm, aspirational aesthetics — neutral palettes, understated confidence. Companion content: "work a 9-5 with me" formats putting the viewer alongside the creator. One-take clips outperform studio outputs.
Doing it well
Alo Yoga Serene stretches, minimalist studio shots
Chamberlain Coffee Handheld one-take videos, casual check-in feel
Aesthetic Lo-fi ↑ Sustained

Cultural moment surfing

Oscars (15 Mar): glambot rankings, outfit tier lists. Harry Styles album (6 Mar): "Aperture" running under cinematic GRWM content. Bachelorette premiere (22 Mar): reaction and ranking content.

Entertainment accounts Reaction and ranking formats around each event

Instagram

03 TRENDS
IG — 01
Stomp-reveal text Reels
Each stomp reveals a new text line. The last line has to earn the whole build.
Creator films centred in frame; each jump shifts the frame down to reveal text. Used for opinion pieces, mini-stories, punchy reveals. Works in 15–30 seconds. Adapting well for product launches.
Doing it well
Product brands Campaign and new drop reveals
Opinion creators Hot takes with a payoff final line
Reels Text-on-screen ↑ Current
IG — 02
Everyday drama
Theatrical gesture, mundane context. Play it completely straight — winking kills it.
The Bridgerton Shuffle: a Regency-era dance move dropped into ordinary moments. The grander the gesture, the more mundane the setting needs to be. Humour is contextual — no explanation needed or wanted.
Doing it well
Dove "Let Them Talk" campaign, Bridgerton tie-in
Nuuly Regency-era rental collection, deadpan execution
Reels Humour ↑ Current
IG — 03
Audience discovery carousel
"If you like X, follow me." Trending audio + explicit follow invitation = reliable growth format.
~10 images or clips representing content style or product range, set to trending audio (currently the Harry Styles cycle). Metric to watch: profile visits and follows, not reach.
Doing it well
Handmade businesses Aesthetic product compilations with direct follow CTA
Lifestyle creators Content style carousels targeting niche audiences
Carousel Discovery ↑ Sustained

LinkedIn

03 TRENDS
LI — 01
Work humour and raw authenticity
March 2026 algorithm update targets engagement bait. Light self-awareness wins.
Posts acknowledging professional absurdities — meetings that should have been emails, feedback cycles — without cynicism. Algorithm rewards comments above all other signals. This format reliably generates them.
Doing it well
Charlie Hills 132k followers via authentic engagement + dry workplace humour
Workplace culture accounts Relatable takes that invite comment over like
Text post Short video ↑ Algorithm-favoured
LI — 02
Personal storytelling carousels
Still the highest-engagement format. But it's personal narrative now, not frameworks.
Professional pivots, lessons from failure, career horror stories. For organisations: amplify staff voices rather than posting in corporate voice — staff-authored posts consistently outperform brand-authored ones.
Doing it well
April Little 260k followers; candid posts on layoffs and career pivots
IKEA Extended carousel format; 40-year meatball anniversary timeline
Carousel Storytelling ↑ High saves
LI — 03
Sub-90-second native video
LinkedIn is boosting native video. Uploading a YouTube link is a reach penalty.
Direct-to-camera, captioned, single topic. Structure: hook → three rapid points → question. Posts gaining engagement in the first 60 minutes get pushed wider — timing to your audience's active hours matters.
Doing it well
Executive creators Face-to-camera takes on industry shifts under 90s
Recruitment accounts "Top 3 mistakes" format with comment CTA
Native video Sub 90s ↑ Platform-boosted

YouTube

03 TRENDS
YT — 01
Shorts for discovery, long-form for depth
The Short is the invitation. The long video is the relationship.
74% of Shorts views come from non-subscribers — primary top-of-funnel. Channels using both formats together grow 41% faster. Use Shorts as teasers; playlists encourage consecutive viewing.
Doing it well
Madeline Argy Short-form to long-form pipeline; 40-min essays from Shorts audience
Education channels Shorts as 60s teasers driving full-length explainers
Shorts Long-form ↑ Platform priority
YT — 02
Structured series and recurring formats
Audiences return because they know what to expect. Consistency beats brilliance.
Highest-performing channels repeat a recognisable format. One-off uploads don't build a return audience. A defined format and rhythm does. YouTube calls this "digital franchises." 57% of viewers prefer series over one-offs.
Doing it well
Quenlin Blackwell "Feeding Starving Celebrities" — consistent format, hour-long episodes
Glitch Studio Behind-the-scenes series pulling fans into the production pipeline
Series format Long-form ↑ Loyalty driver
YT — 03
TV-screen storytelling
Mobile content and TV content are now genuinely different briefs.
YouTube is 12%+ of all US TV viewing time. TV rewards slower pacing, widescreen framing, chapter-based storytelling. Over-produced videos underperform — viewers read them as ads. Raw, unscripted content is winning.
Doing it well
Documentary-style channels Cinematic pacing, widescreen framing, chapter markers
Gary's Economics Long-form explainers; 1M+ subs via unscripted authenticity
Connected TV Long-form ↑ Growing fast

Enduring Trends
Not new — but still working. Durable formats and behaviours worth keeping in your content mix.
Authentic behind-the-scenes
Unpolished process content consistently outperforms produced content for trust and saves. Works across all platforms.
↑ Still growing
Educational carousels
Step-by-step and list-format carousels remain Instagram's highest-save format. LinkedIn equivalent drives consistent reach for B2B.
→ Plateaued
Creator-led storytelling
First-person narrative with a clear point of view. Audiences follow people, not brands — partnering with or mimicking creator voice still compounds.
↑ Still growing
Community questions & polls
Direct audience prompts — "which would you choose", "tell us your pick" — reliably lift comments and signal to algorithms. Fatigue is low.
→ Plateaued
Short-form vertical video
The default format. Not a trend anymore — it's table stakes. Any content strategy not producing vertical video is operating at a structural disadvantage.
