TikTok
What's trending on TikTok
TK-01
"Because Here's The Thing…" POV Format
Creators use this audio to show moments that restart when they were supposed to end — capturing that "I wasn't done yet" feeling.
The trend pairs a conversational audio hook with POV scenarios where someone keeps talking after a conversation should've wrapped. It taps into relatable moments of oversharing or one more thought syndrome. The format feels authentic and invites viewers to see themselves in the scenario.
DOING IT WELL
@bajablastfreezelover67 — Uses simple staging and relatable context (studying with a friend) to nail the comedic timing
Service brands — Adapting it to show customer conversations or explaining features with humor
POV TRENDING AUDIO CONVERSATIONAL
TK-02
Oscars & Event-Driven Content Surges
Major cultural moments like the Academy Awards (March 15) are fueling outfit rankings, reaction content, and glambot commentary dominating FYPs.
TikTok thrives on reactive content around tentpole events. The 98th Oscars, Bachelorette premiere (March 22), and Harry Styles' album drop are all creating multiple trend cycles. Brands can participate through commentary, outfit breakdowns, or tie-ins to broader themes (fashion, entertainment, celebration). Algorithm rewards speed and relevance here.
DOING IT WELL
Fashion & beauty creators — Posting red carpet analysis, tier lists, and "get the look" tutorials within hours
Entertainment commentators — Offering hot takes, acceptance speech reactions, and behind-the-scenes insights
REACTIVE CULTURAL MOMENT TIMELY
TK-03
2016 Nostalgia Wave
Creators are reviving 2016 aesthetics with throwback carousels, Snapchat dog filters, and peak pop culture callbacks — leaning into "2026 is the new 2016."
The trend defies a single format but captures a sincere + silly nostalgia for 2016's Pokémon Go era, flower crowns, and heavily saturated Snapchat filters. It's resonating with Millennials and older Gen Z who lived through that year. Brands can participate through archival content, side-by-side comparisons, or adopting the vintage aesthetic playfully.
DOING IT WELL
@Jr Stit — Posted unearthed photos with "Lush Life" by Zara Larsson soundtrack for authentic throwback vibe
Lifestyle & music creators — Comparing 2016 selves to today, reviving trends ironically but with affection

Instagram
What's trending on Instagram
IG-01
The Bridgerton Shuffle Dance
Creators are dropping a tiny Regency-era ballroom dance move into everyday moments — walking into work, grabbing coffee — turning mundane errands into full drama.
The trend uses Peter Gregson's orchestral cover of Charli XCX's "360" from the Bridgerton soundtrack. It's theatrical, unexpected, and oddly satisfying to watch. The juxtaposition of elegant string music with casual settings creates comedic timing that keeps viewers engaged. Brands in hospitality, retail, or lifestyle can adopt this to add personality to routine moments.
DOING IT WELL
Peter Gregson (composer) — Even the original composer joined in, adding legitimacy and momentum to the trend
Lifestyle & fashion creators — Using the move as a transition or reveal moment in outfit content
DANCE BRIDGERTON EFFECT THEATRICAL
IG-02
"POV: I Fell, But I Saved My…" Trend
Creators lie dramatically on the ground while clutching the one thing they managed to save — usually a drink, phone, or prized item — in a staged fall.
This format leans into exaggerated priorities and relatable humor. It's perfect for product-focused brands (cafés, tech accessories, beauty) to show what customers truly care about protecting. The staged nature makes it easy to execute with minimal setup, and the visual gag invites high saves and shares. Coffee shops have been especially successful with matcha lattes as the hero item.
DOING IT WELL
@Coffeecloud.Briarwood — Staged a staircase fall with matcha held upright, visually clean and on-brand
Beauty & accessory brands — Substituting drinks for lip gloss, handbags, or skincare for adaptable format
PRODUCT FOCUS VISUAL GAG HIGH SAVES
IG-03
Harry Styles "Aperture" — Cinematic Storytelling Audio
Harry Styles' new single "Aperture" has become the go-to sound for reflective, cinematic Reels — mood over punchlines, storytelling over trends.
The audio has a natural build that makes it perfect for recaps, life montages, and visually satisfying content. It works equally well for personal storytelling and clean brand visuals. The track's polished-but-not-trying-too-hard vibe fits Instagram's current preference for authentic yet high-quality content. Brands in travel, lifestyle, and creative industries are using it for soft transitions and meaningful clips.
DOING IT WELL
Travel & lifestyle creators — Using it for destination recaps, sunset montages, and reflective moments
Creative professionals — Pairing it with work process videos, studio tours, and portfolio highlights

LinkedIn
What's trending on LinkedIn
LI-01
Carousel Documents — The Engagement King
Carousel posts (multi-page PDFs) are driving 45.85% engagement rates — the highest of any LinkedIn format — because they increase dwell time as users swipe through.
LinkedIn's algorithm heavily rewards time spent with content. Carousels force 30-60 seconds of interaction, which is gold in social media time. They work best for step-by-step guides, frameworks, case studies, and data visualizations. Brands are repurposing blog posts, reports, and infographics into swipeable formats. The key is designing for visual clarity with one insight per slide.
DOING IT WELL
IKEA (@tolga-oncu) — Used extended carousel layout for a creative 40-year meatball anniversary timeline
B2B SaaS & consultants — Breaking down complex frameworks, client transformations, and trend analyses into slides
TOP FORMAT DWELL TIME EDUCATIONAL
LI-02
Comment-to-Download Lead Magnets
Posts prompting users to comment a keyword to receive a template or guide are exploding — leveraging LinkedIn's +24% increase in interactions to drive engagement and leads.
The algorithm sees the comment spike and pushes the post to more feeds. Meanwhile, the poster expands their network with engaged, qualified prospects. This tactic works because it's low-friction (just a comment) but creates a direct connection. Best for templates, toolkits, checklists, and resources that solve a specific pain point. The key is making the offer genuinely valuable, not spammy.
DOING IT WELL
Marketing consultants & agencies — Offering content calendars, ad templates, and strategy frameworks
B2B service providers — Using it to build email lists and qualify leads based on who comments
LEAD GEN ALGORITHM HACK ENGAGEMENT BAIT
LI-03
Authentic Personal Storytelling Over Corporate Polish
Personal profiles get 2.75x more impressions and 5x more engagement than company pages — the algorithm now prioritizes authentic human-to-human connections.
LinkedIn's 2026 algorithm is actively demoting overly polished corporate content and rewarding real voice, expertise, and storytelling. Posts with personal reflection, work anecdotes, and lessons learned consistently outperform dry promotional content. Brands are activating employee voices and founder-led content instead of relying solely on company pages. The shift reflects LinkedIn's push toward being a professional community, not a broadcast platform.
DOING IT WELL
Founder & CEO accounts — Sharing behind-the-scenes decision-making, failures, and honest reflections
Employee advocacy programs — Empowering team members to share expertise and client stories from their perspective
AUTHENTICITY PERSONAL BRAND THOUGHT LEADERSHIP
YouTube
What's trending on YouTube
YT-01
Serialized, TV-Style Content Formats
Creators are embracing recurring formats and episodic series — structured arcs that give viewers reasons to return, building "digital franchises" around their channels.
YouTube's Culture & Trends Report highlights that audiences prefer channels offering structured storytelling over one-off videos. Think recurring series, multi-part explainers, and shows with consistent formats. This works because it creates binge appeal and loyalty. Creators like Druski (Coulda Been House series) and Glitch (behind-the-scenes format) are succeeding with short, digestible seasons. Brands can apply this by creating episodic content calendars rather than standalone videos.
DOING IT WELL
Druski — Created Big Brother-style reality series "Coulda Been House" with limited episodes for binge appeal
Glitch — Launched "Glitch Direct" behind-the-scenes series, blurring creator/studio line
SERIALIZED EPISODIC BINGE-WORTHY
YT-02
Authentic, Unfiltered Content Outperforming Polish
Over-produced videos are underperforming. Raw, unscripted, and human content drives greater trust and engagement — simplicity beats studio quality.
Audiences are gravitating toward content that feels real over highly polished productions. Creators like Sam Sulek (bodybuilder with unedited gym content) and Dry Creek Wrangler School (uncut life lessons) exemplify this shift. YouTube's algorithm increasingly prioritizes viewer satisfaction over production value. For brands, this means permission to shoot on phones, use handheld footage, and embrace authentic moments over perfection. The key is genuine storytelling and personality.
DOING IT WELL
Sam Sulek — Posts raw gym content with minimal editing, building massive following through authenticity
Dry Creek Wrangler School — Shifted to uncut life lesson videos, creating intimacy with viewers
AUTHENTIC RAW LO-FI
YT-03
Shorts + Long-Form Integration Strategy
Shorts now receive 90 billion views per day and act as discovery tools — but long-form is where loyalty and deeper relationships are built.
YouTube is building stronger pathways between Shorts and long-form content. Shorts dominate initial discovery, helping reach new audiences quickly. Long-form is where people binge, share, and return. The winning strategy is using Shorts as trailers or highlights that direct viewers to full videos. The algorithm is starting to intentionally link these formats. Brands should think of Shorts as the spark and long-form as the story.
DOING IT WELL
Multi-format creators — Using Shorts to tease longer content, driving traffic between formats
Educational channels — Posting quick tips in Shorts, then linking to in-depth tutorials in long-form
