Bridgerton Shuffle
A tiny ballroom-style dance move dropped into everyday moments—walking to work, grabbing coffee.
The trend uses Peter Gregson's orchestral cover of Charli XCX's "360" (featured in Bridgerton). Creators add theatrical Regency-era flair to mundane routines, turning Monday mornings into "Lady Whistledown is watching" moments. It's elegant, unexpected, and lowkey addictive to watch.

IG-02
"I Fell, But I Saved My…"
Creators lie dramatically on the floor clutching the one thing they saved—usually their matcha latte.
POV: you tripped, your belongings scattered everywhere, but you heroically saved your favorite drink or product. It's chaotic, relatable, and perfect for beverage brands or beauty products. Coffee shops are recreating this with just stairs and a drink. The trend shows what matters most in a split-second moment.

LinkedIn
LI-01
Carousel Documents Dominate
PDF-style carousel posts achieve 45.85% engagement rate—the highest of any format on LinkedIn.
Carousels increase dwell time as users swipe through slides. The algorithm loves this. They're algorithmic gold for presenting frameworks, checklists, step-by-step guides, and repurposed blog content. Real estate agents and financial advisors use them brilliantly for market trends and investment breakdowns. Carousels keep users on-platform longer than any other post type.
DOING IT WELL
B2B marketers & consultants
Turning blog posts into swipe-through visual stories with tension and reveals
Thought leaders
Using carousels for industry trend analyses and original frameworks
Engagement driver Carousels Dwell time
LI-02
Comment-Driven Lead Magnets
Posts offering templates or guides in exchange for a keyword comment are spiking engagement.
The format is simple: "Comment 'Ads' for my free Facebook Ads template." The algorithm sees engagement spike, pushing the post to more feeds. The poster expands their network with people who want what they're offering. LinkedIn interactions increased 24% and clicks 28% in 2025, making this tactic increasingly effective. It works on multiple levels: algorithmic boost + qualified lead capture.
DOING IT WELL
Marketing consultants
Offering templates, frameworks, or checklists with clear keyword CTAs
Coaches & service providers
Using the format to build email lists and qualify inbound interest
Lead gen Interactive Engagement farming
LI-03
Authentic Video Over Polish
Native videos under 90 seconds with captions and strong hooks deliver 5.5% engagement rates.
LinkedIn video views grew 36% year-over-year. But over-produced corporate videos underperform—audiences see them as ads. Raw, unscripted content that feels human drives greater trust. A CEO talking into their phone about a client meeting lesson crushes a $5K polished promo. The platform rewards personality-led clips that feel spontaneous. Video is table stakes in 2026, but authenticity is the differentiator.
DOING IT WELL
Founders & CEOs
Smartphone-shot clips sharing lessons learned, no fancy editing
B2B companies
Employee-led videos explaining complex topics in plain language
Video Authenticity Founder-led

YT-01
TV-Style Serialized Content
Creators are embracing episodic, TV-style formats with recurring themes and structured arcs.
YouTube's shift to living-room viewing (CTV) is driving demand for binge-worthy series. Creators like Druski launched "Coulda Been House," a Big Brother-style reality show under 10 episodes—familiar structure, creator chaos. Glitch launched "Glitch Direct," a behind-the-scenes series pulling fans into the production pipeline. Viewers prefer channels offering structured arcs and deeper value over one-offs.

YT-02
Authentic > Polished
Creators are ditching highly-produced videos for unfiltered, sincere content with simpler thumbnails.
Thumbnails are getting simpler, fancy edits are disappearing, and storylines have a relaxed pace. It's about creating intimacy with viewers. Bodybuilder Sam Sulek and Dry Creek Wrangler School post uncut videos sharing life lessons. The shift is toward genuine connection over production value. Audiences reward creators who feel real, not scripted.
